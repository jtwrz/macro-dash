Yield Curve & Inflation

Yield Curve
Nominal U.S. Treasury yields (FRED) · Updated daily · No scheduled release
TIPS Real Inflation-adjusted Treasury yield; real return after removing expected CPI
10Y–2Y Most-watched recession signal. Inversion (negative) has historically preceded recessions by 12–18 months. Positive = normal curve.
10Y–3M The Fed's preferred recession indicator. Inversion has preceded every US recession since 1970.
30Y–10Y Slope of the long end of the curve. Steep = market expects higher long-run growth or inflation. Flat/negative = opposite.
Breakeven Market-implied inflation expectation = Nominal minus TIPS. The 10Y breakeven reflects what bond markets are pricing as average annual inflation over the next decade.
Source: Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis (FRED) · U.S. Treasury
Inflation
CPI BLS · Broad consumer prices incl. food & energy · Released ~2nd Tue of following month
Core CPI BLS · CPI ex-food & energy · Same release as CPI
PCE BEA · Fed-preferred price gauge; broader basket than CPI · Released ~4 wks after month end
Core PCE BEA · Fed's primary inflation target · Same release as PCE
PPI BLS · Final Demand; leads CPI by 1–3 months · Released ~2nd Wed of following month
Avg Hrly Earnings BLS · Part of monthly Jobs Report · Released 1st Fri of following month
